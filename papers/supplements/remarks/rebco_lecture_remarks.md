## Slide 3: Superconducting Fundamentals
- Superconductivity emerges when materials cool below their critical temperature
- Allows electrical current to flow without resistance
- Critical parameters: temperature, current density, and magnetic field define operational envelope
- Understanding these relationships is crucial for designing practical superconducting systems

## Slide 4: REBCO Material Properties
- REBCO stands for Rare Earth Barium Copper Oxide
- Crystal structure creates conducting planes that enable exceptional current-carrying capacity
- Material's anisotropic properties mean current flows preferentially along specific crystallographic directions
- Must account for anisotropy in coil design

## Slide 5: Critical Current Density
- Critical current density determines maximum current before superconductivity is lost
- Temperature and magnetic field both reduce this critical value
- Engineering challenge: maximize current while maintaining stable operation within constraints
- Key parameter for practical superconductor applications
